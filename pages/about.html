Who we are

EveryCRSReport.com is a project of Demand Progress, a progressive organization that coordinates the Congressional Data Coalition (along with the libertarian R Street Institute).

Contact information

About the site

The American public needs to know what Congress knows. Congressional Research Service reports are the best way for anyone to quickly get up to speed on major political issues without having to worry about spin — from the same source Congress uses.

These reports are already available to the well-connected — we’re making them available to everyone for free.

The Congressional Research Service (CRS) is Congress’s think tank. EveryCRSReport.com publishes only reports that are available to all Congressional staff. They are not classified or confidential. (More: What is a CRS report?)

Who uses CRS reports? Everyone. Students, journalists, advocates, and even the Supreme Court relies on CRS reports for accurate and timely analysis of major policy issues.

Caption.

A coalition of public interest groups, journalists, academics, students, some Members of Congress, and former CRS employees have been advocating for greater access to CRS reports for over twenty years. Two bills in Congress to make these reports widely available already have 10 sponsors (S. 2639 and H.R. 4702, 114th Congress) and we are lobbying Congress to vote on the bills soon (fact sheet).

Now in partnership with several Members of Congress, we are able to make these reports available and build a platform for a modern way to disseminate this information widely. This website is a proof of concept that Congress easily could be doing it themselves.

What does EveryCRSReport.com include?

EveryCRSReport.com includes {{reports_count|intcomma}} CRS reports. The number changes regularly.

It’s every CRS report that’s available on Congress’s internal website.

We redact the name, phone number, and email address of the analysts from the reports. We add disclaimer language regarding copyright and the role CRS reports are intended to play. That’s it.

Our good friends at CRSReports.com have scraped the web for every report that’s currently available online and have found additional reports.

We also show how much a report changes over time (when CRS publishes updates), RSS feeds, and we hope to add more features in the future. To receive an email alert for all new reports and new reports in a particular topic area, use the RSS icon next to the topic area titles and a third-party service, like IFTTT, to monitor the RSS feed for new additions.
